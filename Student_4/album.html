Music Albums

A compilation of audio tracks is called an album. It is typically created by a musician and distributed in shops. Originally, albums were simply a collection of gramophone records with one or two tracks on each. Although many artists still release their albums on vinyl records, an album today is typically in the shape of a compact disc.

From the middle of the 1960s to the beginning of the 21st century, a time known as the album era, the album dominated recorded music expression and consumption. Although CD and MP3 formats have dominated record sales in the twenty-first century, vinyl LPs are still produced. The cassette tape gradually replaced the 8-track tape as the first tape format to be widely used alongside vinyl from 1965 until it was phased out by 1983. The cassette's popularity peaked in the late 1980s, sharply declined in the 1990s, and had largely disappeared by the first decade of the 2000s.
